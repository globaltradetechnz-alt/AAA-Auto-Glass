What Is ADAS Windscreen Replacement?

Modern vehicles are loaded with windscreen technology — forward-facing cameras for lane-keeping and emergency braking, rain sensors for automatic wipers, heads-up displays (HUD) that project speed and navigation onto the glass, and even embedded heating elements. When the windscreen needs replacing, you need a specialist who understands the technology, not just the glass.

At AAA Auto Glass, we have been fitting ADAS-equipped windscreens for years. We understand that the camera housing must be precisely repositioned, the rain sensor gel pad must be correctly seated, and HUD glass requires the exact reflective coating to function. We only fit OEM-equivalent glass that is compatible with your vehicle's sensor systems.

ADAS Camera Systems We Handle

  • Lane-keeping assist cameras — mounted centrally behind the rear-view mirror. Must be realigned to factory spec after glass replacement.
  • Emergency braking / collision warning — forward-facing radar and camera systems that rely on an unobstructed view through the windscreen.
  • Adaptive cruise control — some systems use the windscreen-mounted camera for distance monitoring.
  • Traffic sign recognition — reads speed limits and signs through the forward camera.

Rain Sensor & HUD Integration

Rain sensors are mounted on the inner surface of the windscreen and detect water droplets to trigger automatic wipers. When replacing the glass, the sensor gel pad must be carefully transferred to the new screen and seated correctly. We test the sensor before we leave to make sure your wipers respond properly.

Heads-Up Display (HUD) windscreens have a special reflective coating in the driver's line of sight. Standard glass will not work — the display will be faint or distorted. We only fit HUD-compatible glass that matches your vehicle's factory specifications.

Do I Need ADAS Recalibration?

Many vehicles require ADAS recalibration after windscreen replacement. This is a separate electronic process that ensures the camera "sees" the road correctly. We advise whether your vehicle needs recalibration as part of the job and can arrange it through our partner network. The calibration is usually covered by your insurance.

Which Vehicles Need ADAS Windscreen Replacement?

Almost all vehicles manufactured from 2016 onwards have some form of ADAS technology. Common models we handle include:

  • Tesla Model 3 & Model Y — camera housing, rain sensor, full roof glass
  • Range Rover / Land Rover — HUD, heated elements, rain sensor, ADAS camera
  • BMW, Mercedes-Benz, Audi — lane-keep, collision warning, HUD options
  • Toyota, Mazda, Honda, Nissan — Toyota Safety Sense, Mazda i-Activsense, Honda Sensing
  • Hyundai, Kia, Volkswagen — SmartSense, IQ.Drive, and similar systems

If you are unsure whether your vehicle has ADAS, just give us your registration number and we can tell you what technology is fitted.
